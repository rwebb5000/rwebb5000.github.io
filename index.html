THE EVOLUTION OF MIGOS

The members of Migos: Quavo, Offset, Takeoff

2009 - 2012: Formation and First Releases

The group Migos was formed in 2009, by Quavo (real name Quavious Marshall), Takeoff (real name Kirsnick Ball), and Offset (real name Kiari Cephus).
The three memebers are related to each other and were raised together. Quavo is Takeoff's uncle, and Offset is Quavo's cousin. They grew up in Gwinnett
county , an hour northeast of Atlanta. Their first full-length project, a mixtape titled Juug Season, was released on August 25, 2011. Their next mixtape
titled No Label and was released on June 1, 2012.

2013: Their Breakthrough

In 2013, Migos came out with their hit single versace. The single was produced by Atlanta's finest, Zaytoven and peaked on Billoards top 100
at number 99. Later during the year Drake, a popular rapper, remixed the song and brought more light to the single. On June 13, Migos released their
mixtape Y.R.N. Click here to see their top 5 songs from their album. On June 15, 2013, the group performed at rap radio station Hot 107.9's Birhday Bash.
Versace was included on XXL's "The 25 Best Songs of 2013".

2014 - 2015: Mixtapes

On February 25, 2014, Migos released a mixtape titled No Label 2 and was downloaded over 100,000 times in the first week of its release was certified gold
on mixtape sharing site Datpiff. In June 2014, their single Fight Night was featured on XXL's "25 Best Songs of 2014 so far" list. On February 5, 2015,
Migos released their first single from their album titled One Time and peaked at number 34 on the US HotRandB/Hip-Hop Songs. The album,Yung Rich Nation, recieved
generally positive reviews from music critics. On September 17, 2015, Migos released a new mixtape titled Back to the Bando that featured the infamous song "Look at my dab"
made famous by Cam Newton and Odell Beckham Jr.

2016 - Present

On October 28, 2016, Migos released the first single titled Bad and Boujee, from their upcoming second album. The single went on to the BillBoard Hot 100, becoming
Migo's first number one single in the US. Their second album was released on January 27, 2017 titled Culture. On October 27, Migos officially released MotorSport, the
lead single of their secong studio album Culture II. The second single Stir Fry was released on December 20, 2017. The song was used for the 2018 NBA All-Star Weekend.
